# Niche / Industry Template

Use this structure for "Niche" pillar articles.

## Article Structure

### TL;DR (2-3 sentences)
- Market opportunity size
- Key insight
- Who should read this

### Main Content

#### H2: The [Niche] Market Opportunity
- Market size and growth
- Why now is the time
- Key trends driving demand

#### H2: Understanding the [Niche] Audience

##### H3: Who They Are
- Demographics
- Psychographics
- Pain points
- Goals and aspirations

##### H3: What They Need
- Primary needs (3-5)
- Unmet needs
- Willingness to pay

#### H2: Top [Niche] Ideas / Categories

For each niche idea:
##### H3: [Niche Idea Name]
- **Target Market**: Who specifically
- **Market Size**: Estimate
- **Competition**: Low/Medium/High
- **Entry Barrier**: What it takes
- **Revenue Potential**: Range
- **Example**: Real company or scenario

#### H2: How to Succeed in [Niche]

##### H3: Key Success Factors
- Numbered list of critical factors
- Why each matters
- How to implement

##### H3: Common Pitfalls
- Mistakes to avoid
- Lessons from failures
- Risk mitigation

#### H2: Marketing to [Niche] Audience
- Channel recommendations
- Messaging strategies
- Content ideas
- Partnership opportunities

#### H2: Tools and Resources
- Essential tools
- Learning resources
- Communities to join
- Experts to follow

#### H2: Getting Started Checklist
- [ ] Step 1: Research
- [ ] Step 2: Validate
- [ ] Step 3: Build MVP
- [ ] Step 4: Launch
- [ ] Step 5: Scale

### FAQ Section (5-8 questions)
Niche-specific questions:
- "Is [niche] profitable?"
- "How do I start a [niche] business?"
- "What is the market size for [niche]?"
- "Who are the competitors in [niche]?"
- "What skills do I need for [niche]?"

### Meta Description
- Under 160 characters
- Include niche name
- Mention opportunity or profit

## Writing Style
- Data-driven insights
- Include real examples
- Be actionable
- Inspire confidence
- Acknowledge challenges

## SEO Guidelines
- Target "[niche] business" keywords
- Include "market", "opportunity", "ideas"
- Link to case studies
- Use industry-specific terms
